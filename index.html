Disposing of used pouches and bottles improperly isn't just an eyesore; it can have far-reaching consequences for both humans and animals. Here's a glimpse into the negative impacts:

Environmental Degradation: Plastic bags, bottles, and other waste can clog drains, pollute waterways, and harm wildlife. Animals often mistake them for food, leading to choking hazards and digestive issues. Spread of Disease: Improper waste disposal creates breeding grounds for disease-carrying vectors like mosquitoes and rodents. This can increase the risk of illnesses like malaria, dengue fever, and leptospirosis for both humans and animals. Injuries to Animals: Sharp objects and broken glass in discarded waste can cause severe injuries to animals who scavenge for food or shelter. This can range from cuts and punctures to internal organ damage. Public Health Hazards: Open garbage attracts stray dogs, vermin and creates unsanitary conditions, potentially leading to the spread of bacteria and parasites. This can pose a significant health risk to humans, especially children playing in such areas. Negative Impact on Tourism: Littered landscapes and polluted environments can deter tourists, impacting local economies that rely on tourism revenue. Maintaining clean and healthy surroundings is crucial for sustainable tourism development. By responsibly disposing of our waste pouches and bottles and promoting proper waste management practices, we can create a safer and healthier environment for all. Remember, even small actions can make a big difference. Choose to bin, dispose of pouches and bottles after use thoughtfully to be a part of the solution, not the problem.

Our Service

Binary’s workflow is designed to encourage eco-friendly practices by rewarding customers for recycling used wafers. Customers upload details of the used wafer / Bottle which purchased from any local / Online store and deposit it in a designated bin, along with a photograph. The reward points depending on the wafer type, with 1 point equivalent to 1 Indian rupee. Once customers accumulate a minimum of 100 points, they can redeem them. This system promotes responsible disposal and offers a tangible incentive, making it easy for people to contribute to environmental sustainability while earning rewards.

Core Functionality of Binahmy Mobile Application

  • Product Purchase:

  • 1. User selects a product from any point of sale (online or shop)

  • 2. App displays the product price plus a mandatory environmental fee (minimum ₹2).

  • 3. The environment fee may be offered by manufacturer or collected from the purchaser, and both may be also included

  • 4. User proceeds with payment.

  • 5. Seller collects the environmental fee along with the product price.

  • Waste Disposal and Point Accumulation:

  • 1. User disposes of the product's empty wrap/bottle responsibly at any designated bins (Bin location is also available at app)

  • 2. User captures a clear image of the disposed waste.

  • 3. User uploads the image to the Binahmy app.

  • 4. App verifies the image for proper disposal.

  • 5. Upon verification, the user is awarded points equivalent to the paid environmental fee and incentives if offered by the manufacturer

  • Point Management and Redemption:

  • 1. Points are accumulated in the user's Binahmy account.

  • 2. User can view their point balance at any time.

  • 3. User has two options for point redemption:

  • o Donate to CM's Wayanad Fund.

  • o Convert points to in-app credits or other rewards.

  • Incentive for Waste Disposal:

  • 1. If the user fails to dispose of the waste responsibly, any other user can submit a verified image of the improperly disposed waste.

  • 2. The original purchaser loses the accumulated points for that product.

  • 3. The user who submitted the image is awarded the lost points.

  • 4. Hence the Binahmy ensures each plastic waste is disposed properly.
